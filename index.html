Validation

The Proof of Concept Check

Before committing to any niche, run this 4-part validation. It takes 30–60 minutes and saves months of wasted effort.

1
The Instagram Search Test

Search niche keywords on Instagram (Accounts tab). 5–10 accounts with 50k+ followers posting consistently = healthy market. Ghost town = too small or too new.

2
The Growth Rate Check

Take your top 10 accounts to Socialblade.com. You want consistent steady growth over 30–90 days. 200–500 followers/day means they cracked the algorithm — replicate it.

3
The Money Test

Browse top accounts for 48 hours. Are they promoting in Stories? Shoutout posts for coaches? Affiliate links? Visible monetisation = proof money flows.

4
The Engagement Check

Measure save and share ratios on recent posts. High saves = audience finds it useful. High shares = it moves them emotionally. Both indicate buyers.

IMPORTANT ADVICE

Do not choose your niche based on what you think will be popular. Choose a niche you can create content about for at least 12 months without burning out. Consistency is your most powerful competitive advantage — and passion is what sustains consistency.

The Inner Game

Why Most Pages Quit (And How Not To)

Building a faceless brand is simple — not easy. The content is learnable. The growth is predictable. What stops most women is the invisible stuff: self-doubt, comparison, inconsistency when results are slow. This chapter arms you for those moments.

  • The 90-Day Rule

    No page looks impressive in the first 30 days. Commit to 90 days of consistency before drawing any conclusions about whether it's working.

  • Detach From Vanity Metrics

    Likes feel good. Saves and shares build brands. Follower count doesn't equal income. Track what actually matters.

  • Comparison Is Content Theft

    Every minute comparing your month 2 to someone's year 3 is a minute you could spend creating. Block. Mute. Focus.

  • Imperfect Posts Beat Perfect Drafts

    Posting something 80% good today beats something perfect never. The algorithm rewards volume and consistency.

  • Your Page Is a Business

    Treat it like one. Set working hours, track metrics, reinvest earnings. The women who succeed do this from day one.
